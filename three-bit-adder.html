3-bit Adder

This 3-bit adder is an experiment in visible circuits and visible wiring, woven into a suspended window hanging.

Using binary logic, two values can be added together with a sum displayed as encoded LEDs.

Each row of input consists of 2 bits; a copper wire closes the circuit to represent a 1 or "on" value, while an open circuit represents a 0 or "off" value.

Binary Decimal
00 0
01 1
10 2
11 3

In the example above all 4 loops are closed, representing 3 + 3 or 112 + 112 (binary). This results in the maximum sum of this adder: 6 or 1102 (binary).
